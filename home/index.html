“Truth is, I thought it mattered. I thought that music mattered. But does it bollocks? Not compared to how people matter.”

"It's not an obsession, it's echolalia" That line from Brassed Off has been rattling around my head for years. It’s not because I’m obsessed with the film, or because I think it’s the greatest quote ever written. It’s because of the song. Tubthumping by Chumbawamba has always felt like one of those songs that refuses to leave your head — and honestly, I think that’s part of why it stuck with me. “It’s not an obsession, it’s echolalia.” Some phrases just find a home in my brain. They become attached to a feeling, a memory, or a moment in time. This one reminds me that getting knocked down is part of life — but so is getting back up again. Maybe that’s why this song has stayed with me for so long. It isn’t just a song I like; it’s a little reminder I carry around with me.
